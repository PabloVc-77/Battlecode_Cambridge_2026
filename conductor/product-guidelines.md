# Product Guidelines: Battlecode Cambridge 2026

## Documentation & Communication
We adopt a **Collaborative & Explanatory** tone for our internal notes and documentation. Our goal is to ensure that every team member understands the *why* behind algorithmic choices, and the context for specific bot behaviors.

## Project Organization & Naming
We follow a strict **Versioned (v1.0, v2.0)** naming convention for all bot iterations and experimental variants. Each major change or milestone should result in a new versioned folder (e.g., `trucha_v2_4`, `trucha_v2_5`).

## Coding Standards
We prioritize **Self-Documenting Code**. We aim for clean variable names and small, single-purpose functions that are easy to read and understand. Inline comments should be reserved for explaining complex logic or non-obvious algorithmic constraints.

## Development History
Our commit strategy is **Feature-Based**. We aggregate changes into logical chunks that represent a complete feature, fix, or optimization. This keeps our development history clean and focused on tangible improvements to the bot's performance.
